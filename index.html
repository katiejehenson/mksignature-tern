When the client wants to lock in flights but hasn't decided everything else

Getting a partial authorization (e.g. flights only)

1
Open the trip. For every item the client is not ready to pay yet, click the item → mark it choose later.
2
Make sure the items they do need to authorize have a supplier assigned. Look for the purple banner. No supplier assigned = no purple banner = cannot be authorized.
3
Publish the trip, then send the client the link via email.
4
Client clicks choose later on undecided items → the system skips past them → lands directly on the authorization page → they enter their credit card. Done.
⚠️If an item has no supplier assigned it will not have the purple banner and cannot be authorized. Always assign a supplier first.
Deleting an authorization so you can move items into a package

Deleting an authorization record

1
Open the booking item that already has an authorization on it.
2
Scroll up to the authorization section → click the three dots (⋯) next to it.
3
Click Delete. The signed PDF saved under Documents is not deleted — it stays.
4
Now you can move the item into a package without getting the error message. After the package is set up, send the client a new authorization request.
💡The original signed authorization PDF lives under Documents → Trip authorization (date). Deleting the authorization record does not remove that PDF — you keep your paper trail.
Testing the client experience

How to preview a trip exactly as your client sees it

1
Publish the trip, then copy the published trip link.
2
Open an incognito / private window in your browser (Cmd+Shift+N on Mac, Ctrl+Shift+N on Windows).
3
Paste the link. The system treats you as a client so you see exactly what they see — including the choose later flow.
Why packages exist

What a package does and when to create one

→
A package groups multiple bookings under one total price, so the client authorizes once instead of 20 separate times.
→
Deposits and final balance payments are tracked at the package level — much cleaner for payment reminders.
→
Going forward: set up the package before you send anything for authorization. Doing it after requires deleting authorizations first.
⚠️Set up the package before the client authorizes anything. Moving authorized items requires deleting the authorizations first (see Authorizations tab).
Moving items into a package

Step-by-step: moving bookings into a package

1
Open the trip → go to Bookings.
2
Click the individual booking item you want to move.
3
Scroll down → find Change package → select the target package (or create a new one).
4
Repeat for each item. Leave out anything not managed by the DMC (e.g., hotels on credit card hold only).
💡A warning will appear: "existing entries other than payments will be cleared." That is expected — click confirm to proceed.
Recording a deposit

Setting up a deposit on a package

1
Open the package → look for Itemized deposit and Itemized cost fields at the top of the package.
2
Enter the deposit amount (e.g., 30% of total). The system calculates the remaining balance automatically — you don't do the math.
3
Add the DMC / supplier name. Now the package is ready for authorization.
Showing itemized pricing to clients

Workaround for showing line-item prices

Tern doesn't show individual line-item prices to clients on the authorization page (by design). Use this workaround instead:

1
Take a screenshot of your Bookings page with all line items visible.
2
Attach the screenshot to your proposal email. Write something like: "Here is everything included — the attached screenshot gives you the full breakdown."
3
The client then authorizes the single package total in Tern. Much easier than authorizing 85 separate items.
The most important rule — read this first

Two places to edit — two very different results

✅ Edit inside a trip's Automations tab

Changes apply to this one trip only. Safe for trip-specific tweaks like "Hope you loved Italy!"

🚫 Library → Workflows

Changes the master template — affects every trip using this workflow. Only edit here if you intend a global change to all future trips.

Adding a workflow to a trip

How to add a workflow to a trip manually

1
Open the trip → click the Automations tab.
2
Click Add workflow.
3
Select the workflow you want (e.g., "Booked trips automations") → click it once → it's immediately added. No extra save step needed.
💡Eventually workflows will be set to auto-apply so you won't need to do this manually for every new trip.
Canceling or archiving a trip

Cleaning up tasks when you cancel a trip

1
Change the trip status to Canceled or Archived.
2
A dialog box appears with three options: Unpublish trip / Deactivate automations / Complete tasks.
3
Select all three → confirm. This automatically clears all open tasks at once.
⚠️If you don't see this dialog box, you may have clicked through it too fast. Go back to Automations on that trip and deactivate them manually, then complete any open tasks.
Hiding a trip without losing it

Reject vs. Delete — use Reject to hide safely

Instead of deleting a trip or inquiry, use Reject. The trip is hidden from clients but stays in your account. If you need it later, you can still access it and change the status back.

Viewing tasks across all trips

Two ways to look at your tasks

Tasks tab inside a trip

See and complete tasks for that specific trip. Good when you're working on one trip at a time.

Display → List view

Shows all your trips with their open task counts at once. Good for a daily review of everything pending.

The step everyone forgets

After editing any email: you MUST click "Apply updates"

Clicking Save and close only saves the email to the library. It does not push the new version to your automations.

You must also click Apply updates — this is a separate button that appears after you make changes. It pushes the new email to every automation that uses it.

How to find and edit an email

Editing email templates

1
Go to Library → Emails.
2
Search by name (e.g., "welcome home") or browse folders to find the template.
3
Click the email → make your edits and rewrite in your own voice.
4
Click Apply updates (not just save and close).
5
A dialog will show which automations will be updated → confirm. Done.
Correct order of work for getting emails and workflows live

Do these steps in order

1
Edit all email templates in Library → Emails. Rewrite them all in your own voice.
2
Click Apply updates on each one before moving to the next.
3
Let Kathy know you're done → she will connect the workflows to those emails.
4
Then add workflows to your trips (or they'll be set to auto-apply).
Editing the welcome home survey form

Customizing the post-trip survey

1
In Library → Emails, open the welcome home email → click the "View form" link inside it. This takes you directly to the form.
2
Edit directly — add, remove, or reorder questions. The form auto-saves as you work.
3
To remove a question: click the three dots (⋯) next to it → Delete.
4
To make a question optional: click the three dots → uncheck "Required."
💡Form changes are global — they update for all trips using this form. You don't have to do anything else after saving; the update trickles out automatically.
Auto-save behavior

When does Tern auto-save?

Tern auto-saves almost everything — you'll see the field or name update in the top bar as you click away. The exception is emails in the library: you must click Apply updates or active automations will not get the new version.

Currency conversion

When a client says the conversion looks wrong

The dollar figure shown in Tern's pricing section is an estimate based on today's rate. It is clearly labeled as a helpful estimate — not the final charge amount.

→
If a client says it's way off, they likely did the math themselves incorrectly. Walk them through it: £1 ≈ $1.34 USD as a rough guide.
→
The actual charge depends on the rate when the card is processed — not when they viewed the itinerary.
Reducing an insurance quote

Lowering the insured amount when some flights are refundable

→
If flights are fully refundable to the card (common with business class) or will get an e-credit (common with premium economy), those amounts don't need to be insured.
→
Tern lets you manually change the trip cost to insure. Lower it to exclude refundable airfare.
→
This can significantly drop the premium. Always check refundability before quoting insurance.
💡If only one insurance plan (e.g., Arch Pro Plus) appears, it's likely because the trip cost is above the threshold for other plans — not a system error.
Navigation shortcut

The three-dot menu is your best friend

Almost every action in Tern is hidden behind a three-dot (⋯) menu. When you're not sure how to do something, look for three dots next to the item. That's where Delete, Edit, Reject, Move, and other actions live.

When Finn (AI) gives a useless answer

Skip Finn — email support directly

If Finn doesn't give you the answer you're looking for, don't keep trying to rephrase. Email support@tern.com directly. They respond and can answer specific how-to questions that Finn consistently misses.

Creating forms

You can build forms directly in Tern

Tern has a built-in form builder — no third-party tools needed. Good for things like pre-trip questionnaires (e.g., collecting golf handicaps, dietary requirements, passport details). Look for Forms in the Library.
